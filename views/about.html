A simple boot camp project

When I attended a coding boot camp at the University of Denver, one of our first projects, when learning how to work with jQuery and Node.js, was to create an application where the user would answer a series of survey questions and would receive a friendship match based on the total score of his or her answers. While the project had no issues in its functionality, the appearance left a lot to be desired, to say the least. In the time since I first programmed the original Friend Finder application, my web development skills have grown substantially.

I found myself embarrassed by the look and feel of the original Friend Finder and I decided to revisit it and make it better. This includes more appealing and complex CSS, a more organized project approach, and better functionality. I also made sure to offer more friend choices and better questions, all in the hopes of making a better product. As a software engineer, I apply all of my technical expertise to ensure that the products I develop are not only working as they are intended to, but they maintain optimal UI/UX.
